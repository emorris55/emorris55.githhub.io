FOOD FOR THOUGHT

PROJECT
Haggis, some know it as sheep inards. To Scotland it's a highly celebrated dish that has become a symbol for Scottish nationalism. Traditonal Haggis is the result of sheeps pluck (heart, liver and lung) ground up and cooked inside a sheeps stomach. Robert Burns dedicated his poem “Address to Haggis" which gloriﬁed what was once poor mans food during the middle ages is now the staple meal of scottland. This poster informs the viewer on the how haggis is made, but also captures the essence of Scottish pride. This poster was also displayed in the "Food For Thought" exhibition at milner library at Illinois State.
